Report: act_report¶

Follwoing the Data Wrangling Process which included the gathering, assessing and cleaning stages, the clean master dataset created was then analysed to develop insights.

To analyse the cleaned data I looked at answering a few questions;

In what Year did we have the highest Tweets?

Fig(1) - Distribution of tweets by year; shows that we have the highest count of tweets in the year 2016; this might have been affected by some of the data cleaning process (some rows were dropped such as the retweets and tweets with no images). Another factor could be that the dataset used only had data for up till August 2017 (affecting the count we see for 2017). However based on the requirements and dataset available, the year 2016 is the year that had the most original tweets with images.
The multiple bar chart (fig 2) displayed the retweet and like counts side by side by year. The count of likes is higher than that of retweets but the distribution by year is similar for both cases.

Is there any correlation between retweets and Likes?

Calculating the correlation between retweets and likes using the correlation function, I got a strong positive correlation of 0.79. The scatter plot below somewhat suggests that the higher the count of likes, the more the retweets. This is however not enough for us to conclude that there is a relationship between the two, further analysis should be done to further establish or disprove what this data is telling us.
